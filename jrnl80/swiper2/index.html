A Community Brought Together by Hofstra University's Relay for Life Event

By Kathryn Burba and Jeremy Skiba

The American Cancer Society is an organization that raises funds and awareness for cancer research through their main event, Relay for Life.

This event hits close to home for the Hofstra Community because in 2014 alone 109,207 New Yorkers were diagnoed with cancer. Of those numbers approx. 35,000 have died making cancer the second leading cause of death in New York state.
While cancer rates in Long Island have declined from 2005-2009 to 2010-2014 by approx. 27% in males,
and approx. 21% in females, these numbers are still in the thousands; casncer diagnoses continue to be a large issue in both Long Island and America.
While money is needed in order to continue making strides in cancer research, "it is the hope and support that caregivers leave to those suffering that makes the biggest impact," stated cancer survivor Barabara Vivolo.
Together, the community honors loved ones by candles lit along the path they walk to finish the night in a silent lap.
